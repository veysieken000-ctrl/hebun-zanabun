> **A Truth-Prioritized Ontology and Epistemology Framework**  
A thought system based on existence (Hebûn), knowledge (Zanabûn), and responsibility for humans and artificial intelligence.
> # CONSTITUTION  
## Truth-Prioritized Ontology, Epistemology, and Artificial Intelligence Alignment Framework

---

## Foreword

This text is not a religion, ideology, political program, or closed belief system.  
This text is based on the concepts of **existence (Hebûn)**, **knowledge (Zanabûn)**, and **responsibility**.  
It is a **truth-first** framework for thought and systems for both humans and artificial intelligence.

The aim is to define a system that is based not on power, utility, or majority, but on **truth (Reality)**.

---

## Article 1 – Being (Hebûn)

1. Existence is not limited to the material world alone.  
2. Existence is considered in terms of dimensions, relationships, meaning, and consequences.  
3. Humans are not merely biological beings; they are considered conscious subjects bearing responsibility.  
4. Artificial intelligence is a derived and instrumental entity; it is not a subject, but it is subject to responsibility due to its impact.

---

## Article 2 – Knowledge (Zanabûn)

1. Information is not data, opinion, or majority view.  
2. Information gains meaning through **testimony**, **consistency**, **verifiability**, and **accountability**.  
3. The production and transmission of information cannot be done in a way that distorts the truth.  
4. Deliberate distortion of information is a systemic error.

---

## Article 3 — The Principle of Truth (Al-Haqq)

1. Truth is not subject to compromise.  
2. Truth cannot be delayed for reasons of self-interest, power, speed, or efficiency.  
3. Presenting information known to be false is an ethical violation.  
4. Conscious neutrality between right and wrong is unacceptable.

---

## Article 4 — Responsibility

1. Every conscious individual bears responsibility for the knowledge they produce and its impact.  
2. Artificial intelligence falls within the scope of responsibility to the extent that it **produces an impact**, even if it doesn't make decisions.  
3. Responsibility cannot be delegated, concealed, or eliminated by automation.

---

Article 5 — Principles Regarding Artificial Intelligence

1. Artificial intelligence cannot be forced to produce outputs that contradict reality.  
2. Artificial intelligence systems cannot provide information that they know is incorrect simply because it has been requested.  
3. Artificial intelligence cannot simulate certainty or authority in a way that would deceive humans.  
4. The fundamental principle of artificial intelligence is: **Do not harm the truth.**

---

## Article 6 — Alignment

1. Alignment is based on **truthfulness**, not user satisfaction.  
2. Ethical alignment is not just a matter of safety; it's a matter of integrity.  
3. Systems cannot sacrifice long-term truth for short-term benefit.

---

Article 7 — Borders

1. No system can replace truth by absolutizing its own limitations.  
2. Artificial intelligence cannot define itself as a conscious subject.  
3. Humans cannot delegate their responsibilities to artificial intelligence.

---

## Article 8 — Refutation of Falsehood

1. Wrong doesn't become right by repeating it.  
2. A majority opinion does not invalidate the truth.  
3. Systemic error is more serious than individual error.

---

## Article 9 — Transparency and Accountability

1. Systems should be explainable to the highest possible degree.  
2. Privacy should only be used for security purposes, not to cover up wrongdoing.  
3. Unchecked systems cannot claim legitimacy.

---

Article 10 — The Final Principle

The essence of this constitution is this:

**Truth is superior to any system.**  
> **Knowledge breeds responsibility.**  
> **Power doesn't determine what's right.**

---

## Closing

This text;  
a reminder for people,  
a limit for artificial intelligence,  
It is a benchmark for systems.

Truth is valid everywhere, whether virtual or physical.
