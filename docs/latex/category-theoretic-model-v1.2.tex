\section{Foundational Categories}

Define four categories:

\[
\mathcal{B} \quad \text{(Ontological Layer)}
\]
\[
\mathcal{K} \quad \text{(Epistemic Layer)}
\]
\[
\mathcal{E} \quad \text{(Ethical Layer)}
\]
\[
\mathcal{C} \quad \text{(Civilizational Layer)}
\]

Objects represent structural states.
Morphisms represent transformation or validation processes.

---

\section{Functors}

Define epistemic validation functor:

\[
F : \mathcal{B} \rightarrow \mathcal{K}
\]

Ethical alignment functor:

\[
G : \mathcal{K} \rightarrow \mathcal{E}
\]

Civilizational construction functor:

\[
H : \mathcal{E} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}
\]

---

\section{Composition Structure}

The Hebûn–Zanabûn structure is defined by composition:

\[
HZ = H \circ G \circ F
\]

Meaning:

\[
HZ : \mathcal{B} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}
\]

---

\section{Coherence Condition}

Structural coherence exists iff:

\[
H \circ G \circ F
\]

preserves identity morphisms:

\[
HZ(id_{\mathcal{B}}) = id_{\mathcal{C}}
\]

---

\section{Failure Condition}

If any functor breaks:

\[
F \text{ or } G \text{ or } H
\]

then composition collapses.

\[
\neg F \Rightarrow Structural\_Break
\]

---

\section{Civilizational Activation}

Civilizational layer is active when the composite functor is well-defined and stable.

\[
Stable(HZ) = True
\]
